We were founded on a wager: that the great political questions are, at bottom, questions about the image of man. Name an institute for a general and it will fight; name it for a banker and it will count. We named ours for the poet who held that beauty educates the soul to freedom — and then set it to work on the least poetic problems there are: war, hunger, and economic collapse. Forty years on, in conference halls and concert halls, in reports read by governments and in choruses anyone may join, the argument has not changed. Peace is built out of development. Citizens are built out of beauty.

Why Schiller?

Friedrich Schiller watched a revolution made in the name of freedom devour itself, and drew the hard conclusion: political improvement without the ennoblement of the individual citizen is a house on sand. An institute for peace and development named for him is a daily reminder of what development is for.

The Record

Forty years, one argument

  1. 1984

    The founding

    Helga Zepp-LaRouche convenes the founding conferences in Germany and the United States: a new institute, dedicated to the dignity of man and the sovereignty of nations.

  2. 1989

    The wall falls, the plan is ready

    As the Iron Curtain opens, the Institute circulates the “Productive Triangle” — infrastructure to knit the two Europes together — which grows into the Eurasian Land-Bridge program.

  3. 2014

    The World Land-Bridge

    “The New Silk Road Becomes the World Land-Bridge” is published — the development atlas that campaigns, conferences and briefings have carried around the world since.

  4. 2023

    The Peace Coalition

    With war back in Europe and Southwest Asia, the Institute initiates the International Peace Coalition — every Friday, open to everyone, still in session.

  5. Today

    Five continents

    Conferences twice a year, weekly organizing, chapters and choruses across the Americas, Europe, Africa, Asia and Australia — and everything published free.
