Schiller Institute rebuild · Prototypes

Landing-page drafts

Four generations of drafts. The v3 pilots established the covenant; the v4 series ("the five doors") pushed each theme into its own register; the v4x experimental wing probes the boundaries; the archive wing tries the same page — the /conferences/ index — three ways, in three tech registers. System documentation in README.md.

The archive wing — /conferences/, one page three ways

Treatment I · vanilla · cursor-lantern + year spine

The Conference Archive — “The Gallery”

Five dark era rooms hung with gilt-framed engravings, one per conference (real playlist links). Your cursor is the lantern: frames warm as the light passes; a fixed year spine keeps your place from 2026 back to 1984.

 Treatment II · WebGL (three.js) · the scroll flies you back to 1984

The Conference Archive — “The Firmament”

Every conference is a gold star on one time-thread; scrolling travels the camera back through forty years while a HUD card names each star. The next conference is the one white star. Static index fallback when WebGL is absent.

 Treatment III · GSAP + ScrollTrigger · playbill deck + cursor tilt

The Conference Archive — “The Programme”

Forty seasons as one concert programme in five acts: printed playbill sheets stack over each other as you scroll, the sheet in hand leans toward your pointer, and a utility index closes the bill.

The v4x series — the experimental wing

Door VI · Core axiom · draggable Triple Curve + overflowing bubble bar

Physical Economy — “The Ledger”

The Institute's signature diagram becomes an instrument: drag a year slider through 1966–2026 and the annotation card narrates each breakdown as you pass it.

 Door VII · The namesake · quill flourish + flip-glosses + five acts

Why Schiller? — “The Manuscript”

Literary scrollytelling: the Ode read line by line with German ⇄ English hover-glosses, and a biography staged as five acts of one of his own dramas.

 Door VIII · The parliament · seat-map fill + 7-language switcher + sticky ribbon

The Conferences — “The Assembly”

An auditorium seat-map fills organically as you scroll (one gold seat stays open); the headline re-renders live in seven languages; a dismissable ribbon tests assertive conversion.

 Door IX · Universal history · two-thread braid + verdict curve

Universal History — “The Long Memory”

Gold and grey threads braid down the margin as the 600-year timeline lights — Cusa, Hamilton, FDR — closing on a population curve as the argument's verdict.

 Door X · The common aims · map watermark + drag-the-dawn slider

The African Century — “The Dawn Map”

An engraved Africa silhouette floats behind the hero; then the visitor drags 2026 → 2050 with their own hand and corridors draw, dams ignite, cities light.

The v4 series — the five doors

Door I · Program · engraved score + tuning fork + concert program + nowbar

Classical Culture — “The Rehearsal”

The page that sounds: Ode to Joy engraves itself under your scroll, WebAudio lets you hear the Verdi pitch, the season plays from a printed program, and a persistent gold player keeps singing while you read.

 Door II · Flagship campaign · planetary transit map + counters

World Land-Bridge — “The Atlas”

The planet drawn as one metro diagram: three lines, eight great crossings, hover any station for the real project behind it. NASA night-lights hero.

 Door III · Urgent coalition · sky travels midnight → morning + live countdown

Peace Coalition — “Midnight → Morning”

Opens at two minutes to midnight with a real countdown to Friday's session; the background itself dawns as you scroll. Ten Principles as an interactive decalogue.

 Door IV · Science driver · parallax starfield + altimeter + tokamak

Fusion & Space — “The Ascent”

A vertical journey: a fixed altitude gauge rides your scroll from sea level to Mars while a tokamak assembles itself mid-page. Heir of the v4 hero's Act 2.

 Door V · Recruitment · poster type + city marquee + cursor-torch manifesto

Youth Movement — “The Torch”

A recruitment poster with a conscience: the manifesto is read by torchlight and your cursor is the torch. First month spelled out week by week.
