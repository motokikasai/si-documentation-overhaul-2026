Get Involved · The Schiller Institute

Start where you are.

There are four ways into this movement — the lightest takes two minutes, the deepest takes a life, and nobody has ever been asked to skip a rung.

Scroll

Nobody joins an institute; people join arguments. The argument here is that mankind is one species with one future, that peace is engineered out of development, and that a citizen equal to that future is made — by ideas, and by beauty. If some part of you suspects this is true, you are already most of the way in. What remains is practical: an inbox, an evening, a Friday, a chorus. This page is the staircase, and you may stop on any step and still be welcome on it.

The Ways In

Four rungs, one staircase

  1. Two minutes

    Subscribe

    The weekly dispatch: the analyses, the invitations, the music. Most people who now run chapters started exactly here.

    Get the dispatch ↓

  2. One evening

    Attend

    A conference (free, six languages, online or in person) or a Friday session of the International Peace Coalition. Come once; see whether the room is what we say it is.

    Register for a conference →

  3. A weekly hour

    Sing, or organize

    Join a community chorus — no auditions, tuned to the Verdi pitch — or the chapter nearest you, where the reports become petitions, meetings and town halls.

    Find the chorus or chapter near you →

  4. A life’s work

    Commit

    Membership — and, if you are young and serious, the youth movement: the Institute’s cadre school in economics, history and Classical culture.
