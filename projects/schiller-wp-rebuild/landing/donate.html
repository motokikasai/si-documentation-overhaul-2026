It is paid for, gift by gift, by people who want these ideas to exist in the world and know that wanting is not enough. Independence is not a policy of ours. It is a line item, and this page is where it is funded.

I

The Precedent

Copenhagen, 1791

Schiller was thirty-one, famous, and dying broke when two Danish citizens — the Prince of Augustenburg and Count Schimmelmann — settled a pension on him with no conditions attached. In the room that gift bought, he wrote the Letters on the Aesthetic Education of Man: the founding text of this Institute. Patronage is not a metaphor here. It is the origin story.
