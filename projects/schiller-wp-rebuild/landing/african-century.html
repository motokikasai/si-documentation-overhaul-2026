The Common Aims · Africa & the New Paradigm

The next century
has a continent.

By 2050, one in four human beings will be African, with a median age under twenty-five. That is either the largest buildout in the history of our species — water, power, rail, new cities — or the largest crime of neglect. The Institute has spent forty years drafting the buildout. Come see it.

Colonialism never ended; it changed instruments — from gunboats to loan conditions that forbid exactly the dams, railways and industries that made the lenders rich. The Institute’s answer has been on the table for decades: Transaqua to refill Lake Chad, Grand Inga to power half the continent, rail from Dakar to Djibouti — projects sized to the continent’s actual future, not to its assigned role as a quarry. Development is the name of the new dawn, and it is not a metaphor. It is a work order.

I

Water

Transaqua: give a sea back to the Sahel

Lake Chad has lost nine-tenths of its surface in a lifetime; thirty million people live on what remains. Transaqua takes a small fraction of the Congo basin’s flow through a navigable canal — farmland, fisheries, power and transport for twelve nations. Feasibility agreement signed in 2018; champions needed since.

Power

Grand Inga: the Congo’s gift

One stretch of one river could generate more electricity than forty large nuclear plants — enough to industrialize half the continent and light the other half. It has been “almost financed” for fifty years. What it lacks is not water or engineering. It is the political fight this page is recruiting for.
