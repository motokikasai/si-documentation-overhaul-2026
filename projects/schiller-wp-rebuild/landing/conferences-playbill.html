Archive treatment III of III  ·  The Gallery · The Firmament · The Programme

A concert programme is a modest piece of paper making an immodest claim: that the next few hours have been composed, not merely scheduled. The Institute’s conferences make that claim about history itself. A panel on nuclear war is followed — deliberately, structurally — by a Beethoven chorus, because the whole argument is that political reason and beauty are one faculty. Forty seasons of that argument are printed here. The season never closed; you have simply arrived at intermission.
