The International Conferences · Since 1984

„Alle Menschen werden Brüder.“

Simultaneous interpretation at every conference · pick your language above

Twice a year, statesmen, scientists and classical artists from every continent take one stage — and anyone, anywhere, can take a seat. Schiller’s line is not the motto of these gatherings. It is the method.

Register free See the hall fill

Parliaments represent nations; something has to represent mankind. Since 1984 these conferences have been that room: a general in retirement beside a physicist beside a soprano, a head of state’s message read into the record beside a student’s first question. No delegation fees, no closed doors, interpretation into six languages — because an idea that cannot survive translation was never an idea for humanity. Decisions made here have a habit of surfacing, years later, as policy.

I

The Panels

Strategy, science, culture — one arc

A conference here is composed like a symphony: the war-and-peace panel resolves into the development panel, which resolves into an evening of Beethoven and spirituals — because the argument of the whole Institute is that these are one subject. You leave with the connections installed.

II

The Music

The concert is not an intermission

When the chorus stands at the end of the second day, delegates who spent the morning disagreeing about security architectures sing Schiller’s line together in whatever accent they brought. That moment is the Institute’s entire theory of peace, performed. It is also why people come back.
