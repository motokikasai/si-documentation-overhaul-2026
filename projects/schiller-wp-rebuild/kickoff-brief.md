# Schiller Institute Website Rebuild — Team Kickoff Brief
*One page. Read this first. No technical background needed.*

## What we're doing
We're rebuilding **schillerinstitute.com** — modernizing the technology and, behind the scenes, **reorganizing decades of content** so it's findable, beautiful, and useful. We are *not* throwing anything away. We're taking ~4,000–5,000 items that piled up over the years and giving them a clean, logical structure.

## Why it matters
Today the site works like a **filing cabinet you browse**. We're turning it into a **movement engine** — a site whose job is to communicate the Institute's ideas so powerfully that visitors want to subscribe, participate, and give. Good structure is what makes ideas *findable*, so this reorganization is not busywork — it's the foundation.

## What's changing (in plain terms)
- **Articles stay articles** — the blog posts you know keep their web addresses and simply gain clean labels (topic, region, campaign).
- **New "content types"** give structured homes to things that were previously loose: conferences, individual talks, videos/webcasts, economic forecasts, documents, statements/appeals, press coverage, and people.
- **People become reusable records.** Instead of retyping "Helga Zepp-LaRouche" everywhere, each speaker/author/signatory is one record we link to — and their profile page auto-collects everything they've done.

### The eight content types, in one line each
| Type | Plain meaning |
|---|---|
| **Person** | A speaker, author, signatory, or staff member — one reusable record |
| **Conference** | An event: dates, location, program, its talks |
| **Presentation** | A single talk within a conference |
| **Video / Webcast** | A standalone broadcast (e.g. the weekly webcast) |
| **Economic Forecast** | A physical-economy forecast or report |
| **Document** | A downloadable PDF (report, study, program) |
| **Statement / Appeal** | A declaration, open letter, or call — with its signatories |
| **Press Coverage** | An outside article/interview *about* the Institute |

## How you'll contribute (the important part)
You will **not** be retyping thousands of items. The computer does the heavy lifting and **proposes** how each item should be sorted; **your job is to review and confirm** its guesses in simple spreadsheets:
- **`classification` sheet** — "the computer thinks this post is a Video / Conference / stays an Article — correct?"
- **`category-map` sheet** — "this old category should become a topic tag / drive a content type / be retired — which?"
- **`person-map` sheet** — "are these two names the same person?"
- Later: adding talk timestamps and tidying the media library.

This is deliberate: it turns a 5,000-item judgment call into **spreadsheet rows anyone can review.**

## Ground rules
1. **We work on a *copy* of the site, never the live one.** Nothing you do can break the public website.
2. **Nothing is deleted without review.** Retiring a category never deletes its posts.
3. **When unsure, leave it as-is and add a note.** The safe default is "keep + tag," never "guess."
4. **Ask before overriding a computer proposal** you don't understand — there's usually a reason behind it.
